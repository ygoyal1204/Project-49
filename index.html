Interesting Facts about CAMEL:
  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
  • Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!

Interesting Facts about CHAMELEON:
  • Chameleons vary widly in terms of size.
  • Chameleons can not regrow their tales like lizard.
  • Their feet work like salad tongs.
  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder.
  • Chameleons mainly change color in order to communicate or regbrate body temperature.

Interesting Facts about PIGEON:
  • Piegons are highly sociable animals.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet.
  • They are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Both the male and female pigeon is responsible of caring and raising the young.

Interesting Facts about TEDDY BEAR:
  • A teddy bear is a stuffed toy in the form of a bear.
  • The name teddy bear comes from former United States President Theodore Roosevelt, who was commonly known as "Teddy".
  • Teddy bears are a favourite form of soft toy for amateur toy makers, with many patterns commercially produced or available online.
  • Animation movie giant Walt Disney produced the first colour cartoon film featuring teddy bears – Alice and the Three Bears – in 1924.
  • The first British Teddy Bear Festival was held in 1989 in London.

Interesting Facts about PANDA:
  • An adult panda can eat 12-38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  • Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on Bamboo.
  • They have great camouflage for their environment.
  • An adult female panda weighs 200 pounds.
  • Pandas can climb as high as 13,000 feet and are also very good swimmers.

Interesting Facts about CICADA:
  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  • Most have red-orange eyes.
  • Cicadas don’t sing at night.
  • There are around 3,000 cicada species.
  • Their wings are transparent and can seem rainbow-hued when held up to a light source.
